Water Quality Issues and Solution

1. Low Dissolved Oxygen (DO)

  • Causes: Organic pollution, algae blooms, poor circulation.
  • Consequences: Fish kills, harmful gases, ecosystem disruption.
  • Solutions: Increase aeration, reduce nutrient runoff, improve wastewater treatment.

2. High Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD)

  • Causes: Sewage, industrial waste, organic matter decomposition.
  • Consequences: Oxygen depletion, aquatic suffocation, ecosystem imbalance.
  • Solutions: Improve waste management, use natural filtration, regulate pollution.

3. Oil Spills

  • Causes: Ship accidents, pipeline leaks, industrial runoff.
  • Consequences: Oxygen blockage, toxic habitats, long-term damage.
  • Solutions: Clean spills, enforce regulations, use containment systems.

4. Dead Zones

  • Causes: Nutrient pollution, algae decay, poor water circulation.
  • Consequences: Ecosystem collapse, economic loss, greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Solutions: Control fertilizer use, enhance water flow, improve sewage treatment.

5. High Total Suspended Solids (TSS)

  • Causes: Soil erosion, urban runoff, industrial discharges.
  • Consequences: Reduced clarity, habitat loss, higher treatment costs.
  • Solutions: Erosion control, use sediment traps, manage stormwater runoff.
